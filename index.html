Vehicle Test

Terrafugia

The Terrafugia Transition is a roadable aircraft, more commonly referred to as a flying car. The Transition was designed to meet all road vehicle and aircraft legislative requirements, and was successfully certified by the FAA as an S-LSA in 2020.

For the Transition vehicle test program, I designed, performed, and led static, drive, and flight tests, and performed detailed post-test data analysis. I was an integral member of the testing team in a variety of roles, including as test director, vehicle test driver, "CAPCOM", chase plane pilot/co-pilot, and airborne and ground safety observer. I led safety and test briefing/de-briefing events, and performed post-mortem briefs/analyses. I also worked closely with airport staff and multiple FAA Flight Standards District Offices to ensure compliance and cooperation across testing sites in multiple states.

I also served as the vehicle crew chief, responsible for ensuring the completion of all vehicle maintenance and repairs, and responsible for final go/no-go decision prior to all static, drive, and flight testing. I worked closely with the testing teams and the chief test pilot to document and address all maintenance and repair items. I managed technicians and an FAA A&P/IA mechanic to guarantee all maintenance, repair, and inspection work was completed on schedule, and ensured all documentation and records-keeping was performed. I oversaw, inspected, and performed construction, assembly, installation, repair, and maintenance tasks of all types.

Vehicle Suspension

Terrafugia

I was the lead engineer for a number of the systems on the Transition, including the vehicle landing gear/suspension systems. I designed a robust, light weight system, capable of handling all driving, flight, and landing loads, while simultaneously meeting all regulatory requirements from a variety of governing bodies (FAA/ASTM/FMVSS), and still fitting in an extremely limited available space envelope.

I was responsible for all concept development, detailed design, analysis, installation, and testing tasks, including low and high-speed drive testing as one of the primary vehicle test drivers.

DARPA TX

Terrafugia

The TX concept centered on developing a fully autonomous VTOL aircraft that could be used to transport troops and cargo. Terrafugia's role was to develop a 3/4-scale static ground-test prototype and evaluate the performance of ducted fan thrust and control surface effectiveness.

I led a team of 15 engineers and technicians through concept generation, construction, assembly, inspection, and testing. I conducted weekly check-ins with the contract prime, including schedule and performace reviews. I performed all design work for the vehicle body, including outer mold line CAD scaling/model modifications, construction method development for the body and fan duct, and subsystem integration and mounting.
